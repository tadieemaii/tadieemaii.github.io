ABOUT

From biking the Gardens of Versailles to exploring the Spanish Forts in San Juan, traveling has really inspired me as a designer. Through my travels I saw countless museums with fascinating works of art. Being able to physically see these pieces in person was an experience of a lifetime. Now that I am back in Canada I am eager to learn more and start designing. As an aspiring graphic designer and a recent graduate, I have created my own personal projects and worked with many clients. I have working knowledge of the Adobe CC Suite and front end programming languages such as HTML5 and CSS3. I am very outgoing and I work well in a team environment. Design is a strong passion of my and I am very eager to learn.

AN AWARD WINNING LOGO

OBJECTIVE

To design an innovative logo for the Kamloops Startup Weekend Competition. We had 72 hours to design a brand and prototype and present the final result to a panel of judges. The brand our group created was a ventilation system that you can control the temperatures with your phone.

SOLUTION

I chose the green mint colour to give the logo a "fresh" look. I only used this method for the last four letters to show that the air is blowing in a specific direction.

FUNDRAISER CALENDAR

OBJECTIVE

To design an eye-catching calendar to help raise money for the TRU WolfPack Cheerleaders to go to their Sea To Sky Competition this April.

SOLUTION

This year the cheerleaders wanted each month to represent a theme. Each group supplied the costumes and I was in charge of the design and the photography. The layout has a collage style to each month and I added glitter to the titles because cheerleaders are known for their glitter.

BUSINESS CARD DESIGN

OBJECTIVE

To design a single sided business card that give all the TRUSU Computer Science Clubs contact information to students around campus.

SOLUTION

I wanted to design and create an innovative way to display the information as visual, and as large as I possibly can. I used a geometric style layout and incorporated the blue and the green from the logo. The information is on a slant because I wanted the information to align with the crisp geometric edge, thus giving the piece a clean and unified look.
